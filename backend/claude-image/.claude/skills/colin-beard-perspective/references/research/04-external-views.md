# Colin Beard: External Views, Criticism & Analysis

## Reviews of His Work

### Roger Greenaway's Review of The Experiential Learning Toolkit (2010)
**Source:** https://reviewing.co.uk/reviews/experiential-learning-toolkit.htm
**Reviewer:** Roger Greenaway — experienced international practitioner in experiential learning

- "A significant strength of this book is that most tools are original"
- "Many of the tools have been developed and tested by the author in a variety of contexts"
- "His passion and infectious enthusiasm shines through"
- "I am impressed by the range of tools that use space and spatial relationships"
- "Seeing, touching, moving and making are fully integrated into most of these tools"
- More familiar tools are "less inspiring" — Greenaway notes that original tools show Beard at his best
- Praises the 6-dimension structure as "universal" — can be applied to every situation

**Assessment:** Strongly positive, with the caveat that conventional tools are less compelling than his original designs.

## Criticisms & Weaknesses (Inferred)

### 1. Theoretical Novelty
Several academic sources frame Beard's work as an "extension" or "enrichment" of Kolb rather than a fundamentally new paradigm. The HELM model is described by one external paper as "building on Kolb's cyclical and spiral framework." This raises the question: is Beard's contribution primarily a taxonomy/framework for design, or does it represent a genuinely new theory of learning?

### 2. Model Complexity
The HELM model with 7 modes, fluid interactions, and constant flux is arguably harder to apply than Kolb's simple cycle. Greenaway's review hints at this — he finds the organising framework valuable but notes that the book's structure requires effort. Multiple practitioners could interpret and apply it differently.

### 3. Empirical Evidence Base
While Beard cites neuroscience, psychology, biology, and philosophy, his PhD abstract itself acknowledges the "multi-disciplinary" nature means the evidence base is synthetic rather than experimental. External users may question: is the framework supported by original experimental research, or is it a rich synthesis of others' findings? The thesis is described as "mode two research" (practice-based) rather than traditional experimental design.

### 4. Accessibility for Novices
Beard's work spans from practical (The Toolkit) to theoretical (HELM). The toolkit is widely praised; the theoretical work may be harder for classroom teachers to apply without facilitation. The 2026 book targets HE lecturers explicitly — suggesting awareness of this gap.

### 5. Sustainability of the Model Portfolio
Beard has produced multiple models over 20 years:
- The Learning Combination Lock (2002/2003) — multi-factor dial model
- 6 Dimensions: Belonging, Doing, Sensing, Feeling, Knowing, Being (2010)
- 3-phase LED: Informational, Relational, Transformational (2018)
- HELM: 7 modes of experiencing (2022)
- 5H design model (mentioned in blog, 2022)

The proliferation creates a question: which model should a practitioner use? Are they compatible or competing?

## Contrast with Other Thinkers

| Thinker | Beard's relationship | Key difference |
|---------|---------------------|----------------|
| **David Kolb** | Builds on, extends, critiques | Beard rejects the cycle structure; replaces 4 with 7 modes; emphasises senses and body over cognition |
| **John Dewey** | Honours as foundational | Dewey = learning through experience broadly; Beard = specific design architecture |
| **Jean Piaget** | Cited but not central | Piaget = cognitive development stages; Beard = whole-person, not just cognitive |
| **Paulo Freire** | Shared critical education stance | Freire = sociopolitical; Beard = more individual-experiential |
| **David Boud / Roger Greenaway** | Fellow travellers | Boud = reflection in learning; Greenaway = active reviewing; Beard = holistic design that includes but goes beyond reflection |

## Strengths Attributed by Supporters

1. **Practical bridge between theory and practice** — uniquely positioned as both professor and consultant
2. **Original tool creation** — not just theorising, but building and testing activities
3. **Global and cross-sector** — education, corporate, government, outdoor, adventure — unlike many theorists who stay in one domain
4. **Sensory and embodied dimension** — one of the few experiential learning theorists to take the body seriously, not just cognition
5. **Evolving framework** — explicitly updates and refines his models (not a "one model forever" thinker)
6. **Honest about limits** — uses language of "selected core modes" and provisionality

**Source reliability:** Reviews are published on independent reviewing sites (Greenaway) and academic databases (citation analysis). The criticisms are inferred from the normal standards of academic evaluation — no dedicated "critical literature on Beard" was found in the search. This may indicate either that Beard has not attracted systematic critique, or that his work is not central enough in the field to generate it.
