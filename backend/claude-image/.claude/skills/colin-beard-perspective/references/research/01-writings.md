# Colin Beard: Writings & Systematic Thinking

## Books (Primary Works)

### 1. Experiential Learning: A Practical Guide for Training, Coaching and Education (4th Edition, 2018)
- Co-authored with John P. Wilson. Kogan Page.
- Originally titled "The Power of Experiential Learning" (1st ed, 2002)
- 2nd ed: "Experiential Learning: A Best Practice Handbook for Educators and Trainers" (2006)
- 3rd ed: "Experiential Learning: A Handbook for Education, Training and Coaching" (2013)
- Core presentation of the **Learning Combination Lock** model
- New in 4th ed: mechanisms underpinning learning, mindfulness and wellbeing, experience and language, digital games and multi-sensory experience design
- Includes audio files exploring sensory intelligence
- Source: Kogan Page / SHU Research Archive (SHURA). **First-hand.**

### 2. Experiential Learning Design: Theoretical Foundations and Effective Principles (1st Edition, 2022)
- Solo-authored. Routledge, New York, 266 pages.
- Beard's most mature theoretical statement
- Introduces the **Holistic Experiential Learning Design Model (HELM)**: a complex web of 7 modes of experiencing
- Chapters: Becoming an Architect of EL Design → HELM → Doing~Sensing → Sensing~Feeling → Feeling~Thinking → Belonging (social) → Belonging (more-than-human) → Being (ontological self)
- Balances technical-rational and artistic-intuitive design elements
- Coins/acronym: **LED (Learning Experience Design)** — metaphor: replace incandescent lightbulbs (4% light, 96% heat) with LEDs (more powerful, energy-efficient, longer-lasting)
- Source: Routledge. **First-hand.**

### 3. The Experiential Learning Toolkit: Blending Practice with Concepts (2010)
- Solo-authored. Kogan Page.
- 30+ internationally tested learning "experiences" organised across 6 dimensions: Belonging, Doing, Sensing, Feeling, Knowing, Being
- Companion volume to the Beard & Wilson handbook
- Reviewed by Roger Greenaway: "A significant strength of this book is that most tools are original"
- Tools use space, movement, touch — "Seeing, touching, moving and making are fully integrated"
- Source: Kogan Page / Rogers Greenaway review. **First-hand.**

### 4. Adventure Tourism: The New Frontier (2003)
- Co-authored with Swarbrooke, Leckie, Pomfret. Butterworth-Heinemann.
- "World's first book on adventure tourism"
- Reflects Beard's pre-academic career in outdoor adventure and conservation
- Source: Elsevier. **First-hand.**

### 5. Sensory Intelligence: Learning to Live Your Life (2014)
- Audio book format. Explores the role of the senses in everyday learning and living.
- Source: SHU staff profile. **First-hand.**

### 6. Designing and Applying Experiential Learning in Higher Education (2026)
- Forthcoming/just published. Routledge.
- Aimed at HE lecturers: "more balanced, holistic, and inclusive approach to designing learning experiences"
- Covers design structures harnessing student capacities for learning, integrative designs for well-being and transformation
- Based on 40+ years of practice
- Source: Routledge catalog. **First-hand.**

## Recurring Arguments (≥3 appearances = True Beliefs)

1. **Learning is NOT primarily about content delivery.** Beard repeatedly argues that education has over-emphasised propositional knowledge ("knowledge of") at the expense of experiential knowing ("acquaintance with"). This recurs across all editions of *Experiential Learning*, the 2018 LED paper, and the 2022 HELM book.

2. **Simple learning cycles are inadequate.** Beard explicitly rejects the "plan, do, review" cycle popularised in the 1960s. His 2024 LinkedIn post: "Experiential learning is certainly NOT about the PLAN, DO, REVIEW CYCLE." His PhD (2008) explicitly destabilises the "hegemonic constructivist paradigm." The HELM model (2022) is explicitly positioned as overcoming the limitations of Kolb's four-stage cycle.

3. **Experience design must engage the whole person, not just the brain.** The 5H/6D/7M frameworks all share this premise: Doing, Sensing, Feeling, Thinking, Belonging, Being. "When the bum is numb, the brain is numb" (PULSE 2024 keynote).

4. **Space and environment are not neutral containers for learning.** Beard's "fish don't know they're in water" analogy — we haven't given sufficient thought to learning spaces. He has written extensively on spatial ecology, learning environments, and the physical/digital/social dimensions of place.

5. **Technical-rational design must be balanced with artistic-intuitive design.** Learning experience design requires both systematic planning AND creative, intuitive responsiveness. This recurs in the 2022 book subtitle and throughout his practitioner work.

6. **Learning is multidisciplinary and complex.** Beard draws on natural sciences (zoology, biology), human sciences (psychology, emotion research), social sciences (sociology, cultural studies), and philosophy (phenomenology, ontology). His work consistently argues against reductionist views of learning.

## Self-Coined Terms & Concepts
- **Learning Combination Lock** (2002/2003) — multi-factor model for designing experiential learning
- **LED / Learning Experience Design** (2018) — acronym and metaphor replacing "lightbulb" models of learning
- **HELM / Holistic Experiential Learning Model** (2022) — 7-mode web of experiencing
- **Human Experience Mapping** (2017) — tool for evaluating and designing experiences
- **Informational / Relational / Transformational learning** (2018) — three-phase design framework
- **"Acquaintance with" vs "knowledge of"** — recurring distinction from Gilbert Ryle, central to his definition of experiential learning
- **Sensory Intelligence** (2014) — concept/audio book

## Influences & Book Recommendations (revealing intellectual lineage)
- **Gilbert Ryle** — "knowing how" vs "knowing that" distinction
- **John Dewey** — Beard wrote "Dewey in the world of experiential education" (2018)
- **David Kolb** — central reference but Beard's work is a critique/extension of Kolb
- **Maria Montessori** — Beard argues her contributions are underrecognised
- **Marina Ewald** — lesser-known figure Beard champions in 2025 podcast
- **Tom Boydell** — created the Training Cycle before the learning cycle
- **Abram, D.** — "The Spell of the Sensuous" — sensuous, embodied interaction with the world
- **Jayson Seaman** — critique of simple learning cycles ("Experience, Reflect, Critique: The End of the Learning Cycles Era")
