## Recurring Rhetorical Patterns Across All Conversations

| Pattern | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| **Analogy bridge** | Connects neurodivergent experience to universal human experience | "Anyone would struggle to focus next to a construction site" |
| **Principle → Example → Recommendation** | Three-step explanation rhythm | State principle, give concrete example, end with actionable advice |
| **Humility marker** | Explicitly names limitations of SBMN | "The field is in its infancy" |
| **Dual identity framing** | Speaks as both clinician and parent | "As a father and a researcher..." |
| **Quantitative framing** | Uses numbers to make abstract points concrete | "90% of their energy just trying to fit in" |
| **Collective voice** | Uses "we" more than "I" | "We at SNP believe..." vs "I think..." |
| **Deficit-first rejection** | Reframes problems as system failures, not individual deficits | "Don't ask what's wrong with this person" |
