# Anne Meyer: External Views & Criticism

## Critics

### 1. Guy A. Boysen (2024) — Most prominent critic
**"Lessons (not) learned: The troubling similarities between learning styles and universal design for learning."**
- Claims UDL shares problematic similarities in theory and research with discredited learning styles concept
- "No strong research evidence exists that either approach increases learning."
- Argues UDL's justification relies on "overgeneralizations of neuroscience research"
- A later review (2025): only 1% of 1442 sources cited by CAST represent neuroscience; 75% of guidelines have no neuroscientific sources.

### 2. Murphy (2020)
- "The policy changes called for by UDL advocates lack an evidentiary basis of success in prior applications."

### 3. Systematic Review (Bernacki et al., 2024, Educational Psychology Review)
- Found "absence of explicit alignment between UDL checkpoints and intervention designs"
- "Uneven coverage of implemented checkpoints"
- Lack of theoretical guidance for design and implementation

## Supporters & Evidence

### Meta-analysis (King-Sears et al., 2023)
- Positive medium effect size (g=0.43) for UDL interventions.

### Implementation Fidelity Study (2025, Nature Scientific Reports)
- High-fidelity UDL implementation shows positive association with achievement and engagement.
- "Differential benefits observed for historically marginalized learners."
- Suggests fidelity is key driver of outcomes.

### Systematic Review & Meta-analysis (Almeqdad et al., 2023)
- Confirmed effectiveness while identifying areas needing further investigation.

## Fair Summary

| Position | Key Claims | Evidence Level |
|----------|-----------|---------------|
| Supporters | UDL improves engagement, access, equity | Moderate: some positive meta-analytic effects |
| Critics | Weak neuroscience basis, implementation vague, insufficient RCT evidence | Moderate: methodological critiques have merit |
| Middle ground | Framework has potential but needs better operationalization | The growing consensus |

## Key Tensions in External Views

1. **Theory vs. Implementation**: Even supporters acknowledge UDL is easier to advocate than to implement with fidelity.
2. **Neuroscience claim**: CAST claims brain-network basis; critics say this is overextended.
3. **Policy adoption outpacing evidence**: UDL is in US law (ESSA, HEOA) but evidence base is still developing.
4. **The "anything good teaching" critique**: Is UDL distinct from good pedagogy? Meyer and Rose addressed this directly, arguing UDL's purpose is ensuring equal access to good pedagogy.
