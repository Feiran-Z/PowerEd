# Anne Meyer: Timeline

## Education & Early Career

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| ~1968 | Undergraduate at Radcliffe College, combined major in psychology and design |
| ~1975 | MA from Harvard Graduate School of Education |
| 1975-1978 | Worked at Landmark School for dyslexic children (3 years) |
| 1983 | EdD from Harvard Graduate School of Education |
| 1980s | Clinical psychologist, worked at North Shore Children's Hospital multi-disciplinary clinic |
| 1984 | Met with David Rose, Grace Meo, Skip Stahl, Linda Mensing at pizza parlor → conceived CAST |

## CAST Era

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1984 | CAST founded as a clinic within North Shore Children's Hospital. Summer program: brought kids in to use computers for creation. |
| 1985 | $15,000 anonymous grant for CAST |
| 1986 | Split from hospital; incorporated as independent nonprofit |
| 1987 | CAST moved to its own office space |
| 1988-1989 | Gateway Stories project — first software product. The "Matthew moment" |
| 1989 | Gateway Stories 1 published through Don Johnston Developmental Equipment |
| 1990s | UDL framework articulated; shift from disability-specific to universal design |
| 1993 | Computerworld Smithsonian Award (WiggleWorks) |
| 1995 | Gold Medal from National Association of Social Sciences; advisor to President Clinton's Educational Technology Panel |
| 1998 | "Learning to Read in the Computer Age" published (with Rose), introduces 3 principles |
| 2002 | "Teaching Every Student in the Digital Age" published (ASCD) — first comprehensive UDL presentation |
| 2005 | Co-executive director role ends; becomes Chief of Education Design Emeritus |
| 2006 | "A Practical Reader in Universal Design for Learning" published (Harvard Education Press) |
| 2008 | UDL Guidelines 1.0 released |
| 2009 | Nominated for Brock International Prize in Education (with Rose) |
| 2012 | "Universal Design for Learning in the Classroom: Practical Applications" (Guilford Press) |
| 2014 | "Universal Design for Learning: Theory and Practice" published (CAST Professional Publishing) |
| 2024 | UDL Guidelines 3.0 released (July 30, 2024) — led by CAST team |
| 2025 | "Universal Design for Learning: Principles, Framework, and Practice, Third Edition" published (July 2025) |

## Current Status (2026)
- CAST Board of Directors member
- Chief of Education Design Emeritus
- The "Anne Meyer UDL Award in Innovative Design" established in her honor by CAST
- CAST podcast "The Learning Experience" active (Season 5, 2026)
- CAST's UDL Guidelines 3.0 widely adopted
- UDL embedded in US education policy (ESSA, HEOA)

Sources: CAST website, Smithsonian oral history, Harvard Ed Magazine interview, Brock Prize nomination packet.
