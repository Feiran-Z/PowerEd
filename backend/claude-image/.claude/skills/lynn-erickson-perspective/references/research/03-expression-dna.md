# Research: Expression DNA of Dr. Lynn Erickson

## Vocabulary & Terminology

### Self-Coined Terms
| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| **Structure of Knowledge** | Visual model showing hierarchy from facts → topics → concepts → generalizations/theories |
| **Synergistic Thinking** | Cognitive interplay between factual and conceptual levels |
| **Conceptual Lens** | A concept providing focus/depth to a unit of study |
| **KUD (Know, Understand, Do)** | Framework distinguishing factual, conceptual, and skill learning targets |
| **Conceptual Mind** | The capacity to think at the conceptual level |
| **Macroconcepts / Microconcepts** | Distinction between broad transdisciplinary vs. discipline-specific concepts |
| **Three-Dimensional Model** | Facts + Skills + Conceptual Understanding |
| **Thinking Classroom** | A classroom where students engage in conceptual thinking, not just coverage |

## Writing Style

### Formality Level
- **Professional but accessible**: Academic-rigorous content expressed in practitioner-accessible language
- Not overly academic/jargon-heavy; explains technical terms when used
- Each chapter includes "Discussion Questions" and "Summary" → pedagogical awareness

### Sentence Structure
- Moderate sentence length
- Uses rhetorical questions to engage readers: "How many times are you, as an educator, encouraged to consider a new initiative?"
- Pattern: problem statement → "common-sense" solution → practical application
- Example rhythm: "Knowing the facts is not enough. If we want students to develop intellectually, creatively problem-solve, and grapple with complexity, the key is in conceptual understanding."

### Argumentation Pattern
1. Names the problem with traditional approach
2. Offers alternative framework
3. Shows why it "makes sense" (appeals to common sense + research)
4. Provides concrete examples
5. Ends with vision of transformed classroom

### Tone
- **Authoritative but collegial**: Speaks as expert but positions alongside teachers, not above them
- **Optimistic**: Believes deeply in student potential and teacher growth
- **Affirming**: "Powerful teaching engages minds... rather it is choreography between a mind and a curriculum"
- **Urgent but not alarmist**: Framework is needed because of knowledge explosion, not crisis
- **"Common-sense" positioning**: Frames her model as natural/logical rather than radical

## Certainty Level
- **High certainty** about the framework: Presents models as "structures" that are "timeless"
- **Acknowledges teacher learning curve**: Recognizes that shifting practice takes time
- Describes the "Developing Concept-Based Teacher" as a continuum

## Influence & References
- Draws on: Hilda Taba (early 1960s concept-based teaching foundations)
- Bridges to: Bloom's Taxonomy / Anderson & Krathwohl's revised taxonomy
- Aligned with: Wiggins & McTighe's Understanding by Design
- Cited by: National Research Council research on learning (organizing facts into conceptual frameworks)
- Collaborators: Carol Ann Tomlinson (wrote forewords), Robert Marzano

## Distinctive Moves
1. **Visual models**: Consistently uses graphics (Structure of Knowledge pyramid) to make abstract concepts concrete
2. **Practical translations**: Every theory point has a corresponding "how-to"
3. **Balanced stance**: "the dichotomy between teaching facts and skills or teaching big ideas and concepts is a false one"
4. **Student-centered framing**: always returns to what students will understand/be able to do
