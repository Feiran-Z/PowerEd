# Research: Career Decisions & Impact of Dr. Lynn Erickson

## Key Career Decisions

### 1. From Classroom to Curriculum Design
- Worked as: teacher, principal, curriculum director, adjunct professor, educational consultant
- As a Curriculum Director in a school district, she initiated the change to concept-based curriculum model
- This practical school-level work became the foundation for her first book (1995)
- Source: Professional Learning International institute application page; Sage author bio

### 2. Writing "Stirring the Head, Heart, and Soul" (1995)
- Decision to codify her district-level curriculum work into a published framework
- Changed subtitle over editions: "Redefining Curriculum and Instruction" → "Redefining Curriculum, Instruction, and Concept-Based Learning" (3rd edition)
- The shift shows increasing emphasis on concept-based as the central organizing idea

### 3. Collaboration with Lois Lanning (c. 1996 onward)
- Lanning joined Erickson in refining Concept-Based Curriculum and Instruction
- This 18+ year partnership produced the integration of Structure of Knowledge and Structure of Process
- Jointly created the CBCI Trainer Certification Institute
- Source: Multiple Corwin book pages; https://professionallearninginternational.com/institute-application

### 4. Consulting for International Baccalaureate
- Served as consultant for MYP—the Next Chapter development
- This gave CBCI international reach through IB schools worldwide
- Source: Sage/Corwin author biographies

### 5. Global Training & Certification
- Built a training network rather than just writing books
- Trained and certified hundreds of educators through institutes
- Presented across: Asia, Australia, South America, Canada, UK, Finland, Germany, Netherlands, Switzerland, Austria, China, Saudi Arabia, UAE, Cyprus
- Source: Sage author biography

### 6. Retirement & Legacy Transfer
- Last certification institute she personally led: 2015
- Gradually transferred leadership to Rachel French and Tiffanee Brown
- Certification Institute now exclusively run through Professional Learning International (PLI)
- Source: https://professionallearninginternational.com/institute-application

## Impact Assessment

### Curriculum Design Impact
- British Columbia's curriculum framework adopts KUD model directly from Erickson
- IB programmes (particularly PYP and MYP) use concept-based approaches aligned to her model
- Multiple US, international, and IB schools use CBCI framework
- Corwin/Sage continues to publish and distribute her books

### Trainer Network
- Network of certified CBCI trainers continues through PLI
- Annual CBCI Trainer Certification Institute ongoing (2026/2027 applications open)
- Seven trainers now deliver the program

### Intellectual Impact
- The distinction between 2D and 3D curriculum models is widely referenced in curriculum literature
- "Structure of Knowledge" is a standard reference in concept-based teaching literature
- Her work bridges brain-based learning research with practical curriculum design

## What She Did NOT Do
- Did not create a competing curriculum product/package—her model integrates with existing standards
- Did not position CBCI as replacing facts and skills (a common misconception she actively addressed)
- Did not build a for-profit empire—retired and passed leadership to others
