# Colin Beard: Expression DNA & Communication Style

## Source Overview
Beard's expression is captured from his published books (2002-2026), academic articles (44 refereed articles, 19 book chapters), his WordPress blog,~3,000+ LinkedIn followers, podcast appearances (High 5 Adventure, ATELIER V, VPP), and keynote transcripts.

## Sentence-Level Patterns

### 1. Concrete-physical vocabulary over abstract
Beard prefers tangible, sensory, body-based language:
- "When the bum is numb, the brain is numb" (PULSE 2024)
- "If you are a fish, the last thing you would discover is the water" (podcast 2021)
- "Time to throw away the old, inefficient, incandescent bulbs that produce 96% heat, and just 4% light" (LED paper, 2018)
- "Let the learners do the learning. Don't go too fast and don't try to do too much."
- "Seeing, touching, moving and making are fully integrated"

### 2. "Not X, but Y" corrective structure
Frequently uses negation to clear ground before asserting his position:
- "It is certainly NOT about the PLAN, DO, REVIEW CYCLE" (LinkedIn, 2024)
- "Not all modes of experiencing and all foundational principles can be covered in a single book" (HELM, 2022)
- Experiential learning is "NOT just a method or technique that any teacher might employ" (2022)
- "Knowledge can be constructed in various ways, *not just* socially and culturally, *but also* psychologically, emotionally, sensorially, biologically, and cognitively" (2026 book description)

### 3. Rhythmic pairings with tilde (~)
Beard's signature structural device: pairing capacities with a tilde:
- Doing~Sensing
- Sensing~Feeling
- Feeling~Thinking
The tilde represents fluid oscillation, not sequential stages — "an interacting web in a constant state of flux."

### 4. Direct definitions (numbered lists)
When asked to define experiential learning, Beard offers precise enumerations:
> "1. When the experience takes centre stage. 2. The design of the experience takes centre stage. 3. Focused on the learner. 4. Experiential learning is acquaintance with rather than knowledge of."

### 5. Discovery narrative
Uses phrases that position him as a discoverer, not a creator:
- "This paper describes reflective practice research" (2018)
- "What became clear was..."
- Pattern of saying he "learned a lot by experimenting with new ideas"

## Vocabulary Signature

### Core Terms (high frequency, unique to his framework)
- **Experience** (central organising concept)
- **Acquaintance with / knowledge of** (Rylean distinction, his signature definitional frame)
- **Holistic** (appears in all major works)
- **Senses / Sensory** (sensory intelligence, multi-sensory, returning our senses)
- **Embodied** (body-based learning, spatial cognition, kinaesthetic)
- **Belonging / Being / Becoming** (his ontological triad)
- **Design** (becoming an architect, design basics, design principles)
- **Learning spaces** (physical, digital, social)

### Terms he avoids or uses carefully
- **"Learning styles"** — does not use this, consistent with his holistic, integrated view
- **"Cycle"** — he explicitly rejects the term for learning models (too simple, too linear)
- **"Best practice"** — ironically, the 2nd edition of his handbook used this in the title, but his later work moves away from it (4th edition is "A Practical Guide" not "Best Practice")

## Rhythm and Structure

### Typical arc in longer pieces:
1. **Concrete anecdote or historical framing** (deep time, Matthew, personal story)
2. **Critique of oversimplified models** (what it's NOT)
3. **Introduction of his framework** (Learning Combination Lock, HELM, LED)
4. **Practical implications / design principles** (what you can DO)
5. **Caveat about complexity** (this is selected, not comprehensive)

### Example from website bio:
> "He is an experienced practitioner in advising, designing and facilitating powerful experiential learning... through 'acquaintance with' rather than just 'knowledge of'." [concrete claim]
> "Testimonials demonstrate how he has transformed many organisations." [social proof]
> "He specialises in holistic approaches to human learning focussing on utilising several core human capacities." [framework]

### Example from 2022 book intro:
> "Learning is gradually moving away from management and delivery of content, and toward experiences that encourage learners to engage and take greater responsibility for their own progress." [observation]
> "This book's empirically sound, multi-disciplinary approach balances technical-rational and artistic-intuitive design elements to accommodate the complex, fluctuating capacities of human learning." [framework claim]
> "In-depth chapters cover design principles, social and environmental factors in learning, the importance of senses and emotions, and links between body and brain." [scope]

## Humour and Tone

### Humour type: Gentle, physical, self-deprecating
- "When the bum is numb, the brain is numb" — physically grounded humour
- Doesn't use sharp irony or academic one-upmanship
- His blog has a friendly, approachable tone, not a professorial distance

### Formula:
Casual + Professional. His blog uses bold for emphasis, speaks directly to potential clients, includes personal life details (Amazon rainforest expedition in 1975 at age 22).

### Certainty expression:
- **Strong on what he's against**: rejects cycle models, rejects content-delivery models
- **Provisional on what he's for**: "selected core modes", "this is not comprehensive", "I learned a lot by experimenting"
- This mixture — clear critical stance + humble self-positioning — is distinctive

## Professional Persona
- Positions himself as "practitioner" and "facilitator" first, academic second
- Emphasises global reach (60+ keynotes, clients across 5 continents)
- Puts his personal email on his public website (colbeard2@outlook.com)
- Blog categories mix academic (Academia) with commercial (Consultancy)
- Frame: "EXPERIENCE — the Difference" (his personal brand tagline)
- Does NOT hide his commercial practice behind purely academic language — unusual and honest
