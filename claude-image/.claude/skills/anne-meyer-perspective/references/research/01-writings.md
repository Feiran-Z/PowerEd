# Anne Meyer: Writings & Systematic Thinking

## Books (first-hand, highest credibility)

1. **Universal Design for Learning: Theory and Practice** (2014) — Lead author with David Rose and David Gordon. CAST Professional Publishing. The first comprehensive UDL statement since 2002. Multimedia, interactive format.
   - Core thesis: learning environments should be designed from the start for variability, not retrofitted.

2. **Universal Design for Learning: Principles, Framework, and Practice, Third Edition** (2025) — Latest update, new chapters on UDL Guidelines 3.0. ISBN: 9781943085392.

3. **Teaching Every Student in the Digital Age: Universal Design for Learning** (2002) — Seminal work, first comprehensive presentation of UDL principles to a broad audience. ASCD.

4. **A Practical Reader in Universal Design for Learning** (2006) — Co-edited with David Rose. Harvard Education Press.

5. **Learning to Read in the Computer Age** (1998) — Co-authored with David Rose. Brookline Books. Introduced three basic principles that would become UDL framework. Commissioned by reading scholar Jeanne Chall.

6. **The Universally Designed Classroom: Accessible Curriculum and Digital Technologies** (2005) — Co-editor with David Rose and Chuck Hitchcock. Harvard Education Press.

7. **Universal Design for Learning in the Classroom: Practical Applications** (2012) — Co-editor with Tracey Hall and David Rose. Guilford Press.

## Key Articles

- "Universal Design in the Classroom: Do it Once, Do it Right" (1998, Edutopia) — Translated architectural universal design concept to curriculum design.
- "The Future Is in the Margins: The Role of Technology and Disability in Educational Reform" (with Rose, 2008) — Argued that innovations for marginalized students drive reform for everyone.
- "Smart Uses of the Smart Machine: Computers and Your Child's Learning" (1998) — Co-authored with Bart Pisha.

## Recurring Core Arguments (3+ appearances = true beliefs)

1. **Barriers are in the curriculum, not the student** — The defining shift in Meyer's thinking from clinical to educational design. Appears in every book.
2. **Learner variability is systematic and predictable** — Not random. Organizable into three brain networks (affective, recognition, strategic).
3. **Technology enables but doesn't define UDL** — UDL principles can be applied without digital tech, though tech makes it easier.
4. **What helps marginalized learners helps everyone** — The "curb-cut effect" for education.
5. **Design for the edges, benefit the center** — Start with the most extreme needs, the resulting design works for all.

## Self-Coined Terms

- **Universal Design for Learning (UDL)** — Co-created with David Rose
- **Expert learners** — Goal of UDL: learners who are purposeful, motivated, resourceful, knowledgeable, strategic, goal-directed
- **Learner variability** vs "learning styles" — A deliberate distinction she maintained
- **Affective/Recognition/Strategic networks** — The three-brain-network model grounding UDL in neuroscience
