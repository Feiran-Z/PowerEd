# Anne Meyer: Decisions & Actions

## Founding of CAST (1984)

**Context**: Five clinicians from North Shore Children's Hospital — Anne Meyer, David Rose, Grace Meo, Skip Stahl, and Linda Mensing — met at a pizza parlor and conceived CAST. They were frustrated with the limited impact of clinical evaluations (writing reports that schools couldn't or wouldn't implement).

**Decision**: Instead of continuing as clinicians, they chose to build an R&D organization that would create actual tools and curricula.

## The "Off The Hospital" Decision (1986)

**Context**: Hospital's for-profit management company wanted CAST to become a high-volume clinical service, cutting research budget and redirecting focus.

**Decision**: Rather than fire someone as the hospital demanded, the team "all cut back by a small amount in terms of what we were paid and we kept everybody on." They then incorporated as an independent nonprofit, splitting from the hospital.

**Significance**: Meyer prioritized organizational mission and culture over financial security. The decision to leave institutional shelter and become independent shaped CAST's character.

## Shift from Assistive Technology to Universal Design (Late 1980s-1990s)

**Context**: Early work was creating custom assistive technology for individual children. The Gateway Stories project (1989) digitized Matthew's transformers book — and Meyer realized the access features were useful for all children, not just those with disabilities.

**Decision**: Reframe from "making tools for disabled kids" to "designing flexible curricula for everyone." This was the conceptual birth of UDL.

**Significance**: She could have stayed in the assistive-tech niche (safer, clearer funding). Going "universal" was bolder and harder to fund.

## Co-Executive Directors with David Rose (1984-2005)

**Decision**: Meyer and Rose served as co-directors for 21 years. A deliberate power-sharing structure reflecting CAST's collaborative ethos.

**Transition**: In 2005, Meyer "retired" from executive leadership but continued on the Board of Directors as Chief of Education Design Emeritus.

## The Mainstream Publishing Bet (1990s)

**Context**: After Gateway's success, CAST could have published its own materials for the special education market.

**Decision**: Instead, Meyer led CAST's push to partner with major publishers (Scholastic) to embed UDL accessibility features into mainstream curricula. This was harder (publishers were skeptical) but had system-level impact.

## UDL Guidelines Versions

The UDL Guidelines have evolved through multiple versions — 1.0 (2008), 2.0 (2011), 2.1, 2.2, and 3.0 (July 2024). Each version reflects deliberate decisions about what to include and emphasize:
- **3.0**: Added emphasis on learner identity, bias as barrier, shift from "expert learner" to "learner agency"

## Current Status (As of 2025-2026)

- Still on CAST Board of Directors
- Chief of Education Design Emeritus
- Third edition of her foundational book published July 2025
- Studying piano, painting, and Buddhism (per CAST bio)
- CAST continues to issue UDL Awards, including the "Anne Meyer UDL Award in Innovative Design" named after her
