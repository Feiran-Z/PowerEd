# Research Agent 5: Decisions, Career Moves & Actions

**Subject: Dr. Lawrence Fung**
**Research Date: April 2026**

---

## Decision 1: Cancer Research to Psychiatry (circa 2005-2009)

**Context:** After earning a PhD in Chemical Engineering from Cornell, Fung had a successful pharmaceutical career at Bristol-Myers Squibb (1998-2001) and Neurogen Corp (2001-2005) working on drug metabolism and CNS receptor pharmacology. He was on a clear trajectory as a cancer/neuroscience researcher.

**The Trigger:** His son Zachary was diagnosed with autism at age 3 (~2005-2006).

**The Decision:** Abandoned his established pharma career, entered medical school at George Washington University (graduated 2009 at age ~39), then completed psychiatry residency and child/adolescent fellowship at Stanford.

**Alternative Available:** Could have stayed in pharma research, where his PhD and industry experience positioned him for leadership roles.

**Outcome:** Created a unique integrated path combining chemical engineering rigor, pharmaceutical industry experience, clinical psychiatry, and community-based research.

**What This Reveals:**
- Personal experience can override career inertia
- Willing to start over at age ~35 in a demanding new field
- Values mission-driven work over career optimization
- His engineering background gives his clinical work a distinctive systems-thinking approach

---

## Decision 2: Founding the Stanford Neurodiversity Project (2018)

**Context:** By 2015, Fung was an Instructor in Psychiatry at Stanford, working at the Autism and Developmental Disabilities Clinic. The neurodiversity movement was gaining momentum but had no institutional home at Stanford.

**The Decision:** Founded SNP as a formal institutional initiative, bringing together clinical services, research, education, and employment programs under one umbrella.

**Start:** November 2017 — Neurodiversity Special Interest Group (23 attendees at first meeting)
**Formal launch:** 2018

**Alternative:** Could have remained a traditional academic psychiatrist focusing solely on basic neuroimaging research (his NIH K08 grant).

**Outcome:** SNP grew from a small SIG to a major institutional initiative with multiple programs serving hundreds of participants annually, annual summit with thousands of attendees.

---

## Decision 3: Creating a Full-Chain Employment Model (2019)

**Context:** Autism employment programs typically focused on either job training OR employer education, not both.

**The Decision:** Designed the Neurodiversity at Work Program as a comprehensive pipeline: recruitment adjustment → interview accommodation → pre-employment preparation → onboarding support (12 weeks, 5-person support circle).

**Funding:** Autism Speaks grant (2019)

**What This Reveals:**
- Systems-thinking approach (engineering background)
- Doesn't accept fragmented solutions
- Willing to design from scratch rather than adapt existing models

---

## Decision 4: Dual Research Strategy (Ongoing since 2016)

**Context:** Most autism researchers specialize in either basic neuroscience or clinical/community interventions.

**The Decision:** Maintain both an NIH-funded neuroimaging lab (GABAergic Neurophysiology in ASD) AND a community-based participatory research program (SNP program evaluation).

**Resource Allocation Challenge:** "With limited resources, how do I allocate between lab science and community service?" (his own words)

**What This Reveals:**
- Refuses to choose between biology and society
- High tolerance for complexity
- Willing to accept that neither line of research gets 100% of his energy

---

## Decision 5: Publishing the SBMN Textbook (2021)

**Context:** Fung had been developing the Strengths-Based Model of Neurodiversity through talks and programs since 2018. No comprehensive textbook systematized the framework.

**The Decision:** Edited and authored the first comprehensive SBMN textbook for APA Publishing, the premier American psychiatry publisher.

**Strategic Choice:** APA Publishing signals clinical legitimacy — this was a deliberate choice to position SBMN within mainstream psychiatry, not on the fringes.

**What This Reveals:**
- Understands the importance of institutional legitimacy for new frameworks
- Willing to do the hard work of systematizing ideas (11 chapters, 330 pages)

---

## Decision 6: SNP-REACH for High School Students (2020)

**Context:** SNP was initially focused on higher education and employment. High school neurodiversity education was an unaddressed gap.

**The Decision:** Launched a 2-week summer camp using Design Thinking + Universal Design for Learning methodology — an innovative combination.

**Growth:** 40 (2020) → 89 (2022) → 130+ (2025) from 4 continents

**What This Reveals:**
- Identifies underserved populations and designs for them
- The DT+UDL framework shows his willingness to borrow from design thinking (a non-medical field)
- Evidence of iterative improvement (published study, program refinement)

---

## Decision 7: Expert Consensus Approach for K-12 (2024-2025)

**Context:** SNP's programs had primarily served higher education and employment populations. K-12 neurodiversity education lacked systematic frameworks.

**The Decision:** Launched the Neurodiversity-Informed Education (NIE) expert consensus study using Delphi methodology — a multi-institutional, multi-stakeholder process.

**What This Reveals:**
- Methodical approach — doesn't just advocate, builds consensus
- Recognizes that educational change requires broad buy-in
- Willing to slow down and do the process right rather than push individual recommendations

---

## Decision 8: Promotion to Associate Professor (2024-2025)

**Context:** Unusual promotion path — started as Instructor, promoted through Clinical Asst Prof, Asst Prof UML, and finally Associate Professor UML.

**The Decision:** Pursued University Medical Line (tenure-track equivalent) rather than staying on the Clinician Educator track.

**What This Reveals:**
- Despite his community work, values academic research recognition
- Willing to meet traditional academic standards while doing non-traditional work
