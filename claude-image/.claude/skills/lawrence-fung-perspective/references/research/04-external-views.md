## 5. Critical Perspectives & Debate

### Known Critiques of SBMN (Broader Field)
1. **Risk of over-romanticization:** Some critics argue the strengths-based model can be used to minimize real challenges neurodivergent people face
   - **Fung's response:** He explicitly acknowledges this: "The characteristics of neurodiverse individuals can be both strengths and challenges at the same time"

2. **Evidence base still developing:** Critics note that empirical evidence for SBMN is still thin
   - **Fung's response:** "The field of neurodiversity is in its infancy" — he consistently prefaces claims with this caveat

3. **Outsider perspective limitations:** Some within the neurodiversity movement question whether non-neurodivergent researchers should lead neurodiversity research
   - **Fung's response:** He explicitly states he does not claim neurodivergent identity and positions himself as an ally

### Absence of Direct Published Criticism
- No substantial published academic critiques specifically targeting Fung's work were found in the research
- Most criticism in the field is directed at the broader strengths-based approach, not Fung personally
- Fung's consistent emphasis on limitations may preempt some criticism
