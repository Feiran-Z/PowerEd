## 1. Book: Neurodiversity: From Phenomenology to Neurobiology and Enhancing Technologies

- **Publisher:** American Psychiatric Association Publishing (APA Publishing)
- **Year:** 2021
- **Editor:** Lawrence K. Fung, M.D., Ph.D.
- **Pages:** 330
- **ISBN:** 978-1-61537-302-4
- **Foreword by:** Temple Grandin

### Table of Contents (4 Parts, 11 Chapters)

**Part I: Overview**
- Ch. 1: Neurodiversity: The New Diversity — Lawrence K. Fung, Nancy Doyle

**Part II: Strengths-Based Model of Neurodiversity (SBMN)**
- Ch. 2: Positive Psychiatry and Positive Psychology — Dilip V. Jeste, Barton W. Palmer
- Ch. 3: Intellectual and Personal Development in Neurodiversity — Lawrence K. Fung
- Ch. 4: Strengths-Based Model of Autism — Lawrence K. Fung
- Ch. 5: Strengths-Based Model of Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder — Lawrence K. Fung

**Part III: Neurobiology of Neurodiversity**
- Ch. 6: Overview of Neurobiology — Lawrence K. Fung
- Ch. 7: Neurobiology of Autism — Lawrence K. Fung
- Ch. 8: Neurobiology of ADHD — Lawrence K. Fung

**Part IV: Enhancing Technologies and Future Directions**
- Ch. 9: Assistive Technologies and Digital Mental Health — Lawrence K. Fung
- Ch. 10: Employment and Education: Best Practices and Future Directions — Lawrence K. Fung
- Ch. 11: Integration and Future Directions — Lawrence K. Fung

**Thesis:** First comprehensive textbook systematizing the Strengths-Based Model of Neurodiversity (SBMN). Integrates positive psychology (Seligman), multiple intelligences theory (Gardner), and Chickering's seven vectors of development into a unified clinical and educational framework.

- https://www.appi.org/Products/Child-and-Adolescent-Psychiatry/Neurodiversity

## 3. Academic Journal Publications (Neurodiversity/Autism Focus, 2018-2026)

### Key Research Papers

**A. GABAergic Neurophysiology in Autism (Core NIH-funded Research)**

| Year | Journal | Title | Key Finding |
|------|---------|-------|-------------|
| 2021 | Molecular Psychiatry | GABA-A receptor differences in autism | First simultaneous measurement of GABAA receptor density and GABA concentration in ASD using PET/MR spectroscopy |
| 2021 | Translational Psychiatry | Cortical GABA levels and sensory processing in ASD | Correlation between GABA levels and sensory over-responsivity |
| 2022 | Autism Research | GABA receptor binding and cortical thickness | Relationship between GABAA receptor binding and cortical morphology |
| 2021 | Psychoneuroendocrinology | GABA and cortisol interactions in autism | Neuroendocrine correlates of GABAergic function |

**B. DT+UDL Study (SNP-REACH Program Evaluation)**

- **Title:** "A dual design thinking – universal design approach to catalyze neurodiversity advocacy in high school students"
- **Journal:** Frontiers in Psychiatry
- **Year:** 2023 (published online 2024)
- **DOI:** 10.3389/fpsyt.2023.1250895
- **Key Finding:** Two-week virtual summer camp significantly improved attitudes toward autism, ADHD, and dyslexia among high school participants. The SNP-REACH program grew from 40 students (2020) to 89 (2022).

**C. DIVERSE Curriculum Efficacy Trial**

- **Title:** "DIVERSE: Developing Inclusive Vocational Educational Resources for Success and Employment"
- **Journal:** Autism in Adulthood
- **Year:** 2025
- **DOI:** 10.1089/aut.2024.0270
- **Key Finding:** First efficacy data for a curriculum training job coaches, employers, and family members on supporting autistic employees

**D. Neurodiversity-Affirming Care**

- **Title:** "Integrating Neurodiversity-Affirming Care into Clinical Practice"
- **Journal:** Journal of the American Academy of Child and Adolescent Psychiatry (JAACAP)
- **Year:** 2025
- **Significance:** First CME curriculum training clinicians through role-play with neurodivergent clinicians

**E. Adult Autism Research (2026)**

- **Title:** "Neurodiversity and Adults on the Autism Spectrum"
- **Journal:** Psychiatric Annals
- **Year:** March 1, 2026
- **Key Content:** Lead article; proposes 8 themes for neurodiversity-informed adult psychiatric care

**F. Employment Disparities (2026)**

- **Title:** Study on unemployment/underemployment by race in autistic job seekers
- **Journal:** Journal of Autism and Developmental Disorders
- **Year:** 2026
- **Significance:** First study examining race as a moderator in autism employment outcomes
