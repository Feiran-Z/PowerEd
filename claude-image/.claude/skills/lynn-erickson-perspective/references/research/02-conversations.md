# Research: Conversations & Interviews of Dr. Lynn Erickson

## Key Dialogue Sources

### 1. Corwin Connect Article/Interview (January 2017)
- Title: "The Common-Sense Model for Teaching and Learning: Concept-Based Curriculum and Instruction"
- Written by Erickson herself for Corwin Connect blog
- Key quotes:
  - "This is the question I asked three decades ago when I realized that the traditional curriculum design, driven by content and skill objectives, failed to effectively develop the deeper thinking and understanding of students."
  - "The Concept-Based model does not 'assume' students understand disciplinary concepts and conceptual understandings."
  - "It systematically builds conceptual schemata in the brain so students can relate new knowledge to prior knowledge, transfer understanding from one context to another, and personally construct deeper understanding using facts and skills as tools."
- Source: https://corwin-connect.com/2017/01/common-sense-model-teaching-learning-concept-based-curriculum-instruction/

### 2. British Columbia Ministry of Education Interview (c. 2012)
- Conducted by Pat Horstead of the Curriculum and Assessment Branch
- Erickson consulted with BC Ministry on their "Know-Do-Understand" curriculum model
- BC's curriculum model explicitly draws from Erickson's framework
- Source: https://www.curriculum.gov.bc.ca/instructional-samples/know-do-understand

### 3. Rachel French's "The Thinking Classroom" Podcast
- Erickson's work is discussed in episodes on Concept-Based Inquiry
- French regularly cites Erickson's mentorship
- Source: https://professionallearninginternational.com (podcast)

## Interview Style Observations (from available materials)
- **Balances theory and practice**: Consistently moves from "why this matters" to "how to do it"
- **Uses metaphor effectively**: "Common-sense model," "inch deep and a mile wide," "the eyes light up"
- **Acknowledges collaborators openly**: "joined by my colleague and friend, Dr. Lois Lanning"
- **Responds to critics by clarifying**: emphasizes that facts and skills ARE important, just not sufficient alone
- **Focus on student engagement**: repeatedly connects conceptual understanding to student motivation
