## Comparison with Other Frameworks

### vs. Anderson & Krathwohl's Revised Bloom's Taxonomy
- Erickson's Structure of Knowledge is "simpler... and offers greater insight into the relationship between factual knowledge and conceptual understanding"
- Unlike Bloom's, Erickson explicitly distinguishes factual knowledge from conceptual understanding as qualitatively different, not just hierarchical levels
- Source: Corwin sample chapter (Tools for Teaching Conceptual Understanding)

### vs. Wiggins & McTighe (Understanding by Design)
- Both focus on "big ideas" and transfer
- Erickson provides more explicit structural models (Structure of Knowledge visual)
- UbD's "enduring understandings" map to Erickson's "generalizations"
- Erickson's framework explicitly separates the conceptual dimension from facts and skills

### vs. Hilda Taba (1960s)
- Erickson continues Taba's emphasis on concepts over facts
- Erickson adds the three-dimensional visual model and practical design tools
- Taba identified the three levels of knowledge; Erickson operationalized them into a curriculum design system

## Critiques & Limitations (from external perspectives)

### Implementation Challenge
- Concept-based curriculum is "more complex" to design than traditional topic-based curriculum
- Teachers need significant professional development and mindset shift
- The certification institute approach (costly, time-intensive) suggests barriers to widespread adoption

### Potential Weaknesses
- A Goodreads reviewer noted skepticism about whether disciplines like history offer generalizable truths of the pattern "Revolutions lead to improved living conditions for the oppressed"
- The framework may work better in structured disciplines (science, math) than in interpretive disciplines (history, literature)
- Erickson and Lanning acknowledge this by distinguishing macro vs. micro concepts and providing discipline-specific examples

### IB Research Report Observations (2020)
- Teachers often assume they use inquiry/concept-based methods when observations show they don't
- Disconnect between teacher self-assessment and classroom reality
- Most successful with teachers who have deep content knowledge AND conceptual understanding
- Source: https://www.ibo.org/contentassets/318968269ae5441d8df5ae76542817a0/revised-cbtl-final-report-march-2020-2.pdf
